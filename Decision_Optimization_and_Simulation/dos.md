**Decision Optimization & Simulation**   

***Course Description:***   
Use a variety of software tools for implementing operations research techniques to model and find optimal solutions for business scenarios, including those involving elements of chance. Application areas may include production, transportation, inventory, and queuing.  

***Key Takeaways:***   
This course covered many different decision-making tools.  One that stuck with me the most were the probability mass function (PMF) and cumulative distribution function (CDF), which determine the probability outcomes of random variables. The PMF is used on discrete variables, while the CDF can be used for both discrete and continuous variables.  Another one was learninng about Solver within Excel to identify the best soultions for a situation based on specific constraints.  Lastly, we also learned about SimQuick that allowed us to run different scenarios and analyze performance such as wait times and variability.  The assignment linked below highlights the use of Excel for simulating real-world scenarios as well as applying Solver to identify optimal solutions based on constraints.
