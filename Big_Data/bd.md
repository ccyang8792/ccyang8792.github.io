**Big Data**   

***Course Description:***   
Students will embark on a foundational exploration of Big Data, tracing its evolution and understanding the fundamental characteristics that make it unique. The course provides an overview of the intricate architecture of Big data and introduces common and widely used distributed computing frameworks. Through hands-on activities, participants will develop practical skills in data storage, management, processing, and analytics, with an understanding of the challenges related to scalability and performance optimization in large-scale environments.      

***Key Takeaways:***   
For this class, we primarily used SQL Server Management Studio (SSMS) to import, clean, transform, and analyze datasets.  We focused on computing aggregations in SQL and later progressed to using Common Table Expressions (CTEs) to create derived features and categories that provided deeper insights into the data.  One thing I remember vividly from this class is that the instructor did very little lecturing and instead gave us plenty of time to work hands-on with our scripts.  This allowed us to experiment with queries, troubleshoot issues we encountered, and build and refine them while improving our understanding of SQL.
