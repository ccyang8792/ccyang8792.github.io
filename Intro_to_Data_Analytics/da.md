**INTRO TO DATA ANALYTICS** 

**Course Description:**   
Assess data quality and characteristics and apply descriptive and diagnostic techniques to provide insight and answers to
business questions. Appropriately communicate findings. Microsoft Excel will be used extensively.   

**Key Takeaways:**   
This course introduced me to Power Query in Excel, which strengthened my data cleaning skills and allowed me to create new columns using functions such as IFS, COUNTIFS, and XLOOKUP.  I also learned how to build pivot tables and charts to create visualizations that support data analysis and improve the presentation of insights.  In addition, the course emphasized different ways to communicate findings effectively, such as using percentages rather than raw values to provide clearer comparisons.  Toward the end of the course, we explored RFM models to analyze behavioral trends based on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary activity.  We also applied control charts to examine data over time and determine whether a process was stable or if variations were caused by specific factors.
